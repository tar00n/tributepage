About

Satyajit Ray was an Indian Bengali filmmaker, widely regarded as one of the greatest filmmakers of the 20th century. ay was born in the city of Calcutta into a Bengali family prominent in the world of arts and literature. Starting his career as a commercial artist, Ray was drawn into independent filmmaking after meeting French filmmaker Jean Renoir and viewing Vittorio De Sica's Italian neorealist film Bicycle Thieves (1948) during a visit to London. Ray directed 36 films, including feature films, documentaries and shorts. He was also a fiction writer, publisher, illustrator, calligrapher, music composer, graphic designer and film critic. He authored several short stories and novels, primarily aimed at children and adolescents. Feluda, the sleuth, and Professor Shonku, the scientist in his science fiction stories, are popular fictional characters created by him. He was awarded an honorary degree by Oxford University.

Timeline:

  • 1921: Satyajit Ray was born on May 2, 1921, in an intellectual and affluent family in Calcutta, India.
  • 1926: About three years after his father's death, the printing business changed hands and Satyajit and his mother had to leave their spacious house.
  • 1929: At an age of eight, Satyajit joined Ballygunj Government School, until then he had been taught by his mother. Satyajit was an average student.
  • 1936: His mother insisted upon Satyajit joining college. At the Presidency College, Satyajit read science for the first two years and for the third year, he took economics.
  • 1939: This year he graduated and he decided to give up further studies. Even though he had no formal training, he was planning to become a commercial artist.
  • 1940: He joined Rabindranath Tagore's Vishva-Bharati University at Shantiniketan despite the initial reluctance.
  • 1943: Ray started work at D.J. Keymer, a British-run advertising agency, as a "junior visualiser," earning eighty rupees a month. He spend next 13 years here, until he became a full-time filmmaker after success of Pather Panchali.
  • 1947: Along with Chidananda Dasgupta and others, Ray founded the Calcutta Film Society. Battleship Potemkin was the first film they screened.
  • 1949: Ray married Bijoya Das, his first cousin and long-time sweetheart. In the same year, French director Jean Renoir came to Calcutta to shoot his film The River.
  • 1950: D.J. Keymer sent Ray to London to work at its headquarters office. During his six months in London, Ray watched 99 films including Vittorio De Sica's Bicycle Thieves.
  • 1952: Unable to find a producer,He started shooting in late 1952 with his personal savings and hoped to raise more money once he had some passages shot, but did not succeed on his terms.
  • 1955: Pather Panchali was completed in 1955 and turned out to be both a commercial and a tremendous critical success.
  • 1959: He was awarded “Padmashri”.
  • 1961: He was elected chairman of the “Berlin-Film-Festival”.
  • 1963: he was reckoned as one of the eleven ‘greats’ of world as a film director.
  • 1965: He graced “Melbourne-Film Festival” as the guest in chief.
  • 1971: He acted as judge of “Tehran-Film Festival”.
  • 1981: The films produced by Satyajit Ray were exhibited in India Festival organized by New York Museum.
  • 1987: He was awarded by France the rare “Legion de Honore” by France and the reward was handed over to him in person by the President of France himself.
  • 1992: The Government of India honored him with the Bharat Ratna. He was ministered the honorable Oscar award for his lifelong performance just before his death on 23rd April this year.
